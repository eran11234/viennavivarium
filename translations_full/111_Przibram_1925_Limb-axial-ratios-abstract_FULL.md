## Correction

to the paper: "Axial Ratios and Developmental Potencies of the Urodele Limbs, on Models for Harrison's Transplantation Experiments" by Hans Przibram (Arch. f. mikroskop. Anat. u. Entwicklungsmech. [Archive for Microscopic Anatomy and Developmental Mechanics], Vol. 102, No. 4, p. 604; 28 July 1924).

In Fig. e (p. 606) of this paper, unfortunately, in the reproduction of the model a sense-disturbing drawing error crept in, which was not noticed in time before the issue was released and therefore remained in place.

In place of the scheme standing under the designation "homopleural *DV*" for the circular limb anlage of an invertedly inserted transplant of the left side, there was used, namely, that of a correctly inserted transplant of the right side.

On consideration of all the circles drawn in at the left margin of the model, this error can already be recognized from the fact that in the filling-in of the circles the four possible combinations of the black-marked radial margin with the two-pronged figure indicating the proximo-distal direction are not represented, whereas the one combination (in the two circles standing in the middle) occurs twice.

Since the erroneously filled-in third circle is supposed to represent an anlage transplanted invertedly but onto the same body side, the correct filling-in results from a rotation of the normally standing anlage of the same (left) side by 90°.

If we carry out this operation on the fourth circle-scheme, which represents this normal left anlage, we find that the black-rendered radial margin would indeed come to lie quite correctly, as it is drawn in the circle standing next to it, on the side of the circle turned toward the body-posterior (*P*), but that the two-lobed figure in the middle of the circle must turn its prongs not, as is to be seen in the illustration, toward the left body side of the embryo model, but rather toward the right.

I therefore ask all readers of the above-designated paper to undertake a correction of Fig. e (p. 606) in such a way that, in the third circle drawn in at the left embryo margin, which stands under the designation "homopleural *DV*", the pronged figure is turned around, so that the prongs point on the binding side downward instead of upward (turn toward the right body side of the embryo). The black margin of this circular anlage and all other parts of the illustration are to remain unchanged. In the offprints sent out by me I have already carried out the necessary correction by pasting over [it] with a red-filled little circular sheet.

On this occasion I also ask further forgiveness for the not-very-exact reproduction of the illustrations taken from Harrison (a, c, d), which I had noticed at the proof correction, but whose rectification, according to the publisher's communication, would have required new block-making [Klischierung] and would have held up all too much the already unduly delayed appearance of the issue.

Hans Przibram.
