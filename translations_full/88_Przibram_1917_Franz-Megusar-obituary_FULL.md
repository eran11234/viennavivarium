## Dr. Franz Megušar †.

On 3 August 1916, the zoologist Dr. Franz Megušar, known to the readers of this Archive through several works, decorated with the silver Medal for Bravery, met a hero's death as Imperial-Royal Cadet (Officer Aspirant) at Rutka-Myrinska in Volhynia.

Born on 8 September 1876 at Steinbüchl in Carniola, he obtained the doctoral degree at the Philosophical Faculty of the University of Vienna, was active for several years as an assistant at the Biological Experimental Station (Vivarium), and published, in the volumes of the zoological division which appear within the framework of the Archive for Developmental Mechanics, investigations on regeneration and colour change, especially of arthropods. Torn for a while from the researcher's vocation by nervous over-stimulation, he latterly worked as an assistant at the Imperial-Royal Agricultural Experimental Station in Görz. Restless industry and an energy ruthless in the interest of his research ideals were the chief characteristics of his nature. Of his results I regard as the most interesting the demonstration of the regeneration of the totally removed jumping leg in orthopterans and the bleaching of blinded or dark-kept decapod crustaceans.

Hans Przibram.
